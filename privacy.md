# Privacy Policy — My Gmail Grabber

_Last updated: June 2026_

My Gmail Grabber is a browser extension that downloads attachments from the
Gmail email or thread you currently have open — including files that are linked
from Google Drive — into a subfolder you choose.

## What data the extension accesses

When you click "Grab All Attachments", the extension reads, from the email page
open in your browser tab:

- the attachment download links and file names, and
- any Google Drive file links that appear in the message.

This happens only at the moment you click the button, and only on
`mail.google.com`.

## What the extension does with that data

The attachment files are downloaded directly to your own computer, into your
Downloads folder, using your browser's standard download mechanism.

For files linked from Google Drive, the extension downloads them through Google
Drive's standard download URL, using the Google session you are already signed
in to in your browser. The extension does not request access to your Google
Drive account and does not use any Google account permissions (OAuth).

## What the extension does NOT do

- It does **not** collect, store, or transmit any of your data to the
  developer or to any third party.
- It does **not** use any external server, analytics, or tracking.
- It does **not** read, store, or send the content of your emails.
- It does **not** access your Google account credentials, and does **not**
  request access to your Gmail or Google Drive account.

All processing happens locally inside your browser. No information ever leaves
your device except the files themselves, which are saved to your own Downloads
folder.

## Permissions used

- **mail.google.com access** — to read the attachment links and Drive links
  from the open email.
- **downloads** — to save the files to your Downloads folder.
- **activeTab / scripting** — to read the open email only when you click the
  button.
- **storage** — to briefly remember your chosen subfolder name during a
  download.
